YEAR LOCATION EVENT
~9000 BCE Present Day St. Louis People of the Mississippian culture establish a clear regional political center here.
~1400 CE Present Day St. Louis Mississippian culture declines and most inhabitants have left. This area was once known as Mound City due to the ruins.
1600-1750 CE Present Day St. Louis West of Mississippi home to the Missouri and Osage Indians, European settlers kept to the East Side (Illinois).
1764 CE St. Louis St. Louis is founded by French fur traders, Pierre Laclede and his stepson Auguste Chouteau from New Orleans. It would become the center of regional fur trade.
1803 CE Missouri Territory Louisiana Purchase - Missouri earns the nickname Gateway to the West during this purchase as it opens up the territory to settlers.
1821 CE Missouri State Missouri is admitted to the Union as a slave state under the Missouri Compromise. With the temporary capital serving as St. Charles.
~1830s Northwest Missouri Mormon migrants begin to settle in Jackson County, Missouri. Conflicts over religion and slavery begin to erupt in this region.
1836 CE Northwest Missouri The Platte Purchase expands the state of Missouri to the Northwest. After being purchased from Native tribes.
